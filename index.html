why saag?

Spinach is a Superfood

Vitamins

Spinach is high in vitamin A (important for good vision, healthy growth, and a strong immune system), vitamin C (tissue repair), and vitamin K1 (blood clotting, bone health, and heart health). It is also an excellent source of vitamin B6, vitamin B7, and vitamin E.

Minerals

Spinach has significant amounts of iron (essential for the creation of haemoglobin in the blood), magnesium (needed for hundreds of important biochemical reactions in the body), potassium (necessary for protein building), and copper (blood and bone health).

Dietary Fiber

Spinach leaves are packed with dietary fiber, which is necessary to maintain gastrointestinal health and helps protect against heart disease.

Misc

Spinach is a good source of folic acid, which is an essential part of cell production.

Low calorie

Spinach is a very low calorie food (only 23 calories per 100g raw). This makes Saag the ideal delivery mechanism for nutritional goodness -- the Saag takes care of the micronutrients, and we add a filling macronutrient source like paneer (Indian fresh farm cheese, high fat), tofu (soy bean curd, balanced macros), or seitan (wheat gluten, high protein). You add your own rice or bread if you'd like more carbs.
